# AI-Readiness-Public-Sector

## Project Overview
- This project investigates whether governments around the world are genuinely ready to adopt Artificial Intelligence in public services — and what separates high-readiness nations from lower-readiness ones.
- Rather than looking at technology alone, this research examines the full picture: governance structures, citizen trust, human capital, digital infrastructure, and institutional capacity. It combines empirical analysis of cross-country data with a thematic literature review to produce evidence-based findings and a practical readiness framework.

Focus area: AI and the Public Sector
Evidence approach: Combined — dataset analysis + thematic literature review
Countries covered: 45 (across all 7 World Bank regions and all 4 income levels)
Data sources: World Bank GTMI 2025 + UN E-Government Survey 2024

## Research Question
Are governments in different regions and income groups digitally prepared to adopt AI — and what factors most determine whether a country is ready?

## Tools
1. Python (pandas) - Data cleaning and preparation
2. PowerBI - Interactive dashboard, DAX measures, visualisation
3. Jupyter Notebook - Code environment and documentation

3. Geography Is Not Destiny
<img width="931" height="516" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-24 141926" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2f0be71c-739f-4761-89f9-34400de1f0a7" />
Key Insight: Sub-Saharan Africa averages 0.68 — the only region well below the global mean. But within that same region: Kenya: 0.820 (above the global average) and Nigeria: 0.512 (lowest in the entire dataset). Same region - 60% different outcome. Governance decisions — not geography — determine a country's digital trajectory.

4. The Correlation That Changed Everything
<img width="936" height="526" alt="Screenshot 2026-06-24 143043" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/04f85e51-a827-4968-a60c-7cde48563a49" />
Key Insight: The Pearson correlation between Citizen Engagement (DCEI) and overall GovTech maturity: Variable Correlation with GTMI Citizen Engagement (DCEI) r = 0.93, r = 0.49 for Human Capital Index and For Telecom Infrastructure r = 0.32. Eventually, Trust is not a soft benefit — it is a structural requirement. Governments that earn public trust score better across every dimension, not just engagement.
